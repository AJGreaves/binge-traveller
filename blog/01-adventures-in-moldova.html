Adventures in Moldova

Chisinau, Moldova 01/09/19
Air Moldova, direct from Dublin (DUB) to Chisinau (KIV)

I Love Chisinau

I wanted to visit someplace a little different and I had toyed with the idea of Moldova for a few months before finally booking it. I flew direct from Dublin to Chisinau with Air Moldova. They have a nonstop flight which is overnight and gets you into Chisinau at about 5am local time. It is a visa free country for Irish nationals, you get your visa stamped on arrival and you are good to go! You can exchange currency at the airport and you will be using the Moldovan Lei. Hop in a taxi and it’s about a 30 minute drive to the city centre.

I stayed at City Park Hotel which is slap bang in the city centre, right next to Cathedral Park. From this hotel, you are right next to 2 of the main things to see: The Metropolitan Cathedral “Nativity of the Lord” and The Triumphal Arch. Within a short walk from this central location, you should also try to spot some Communist-era buildings like the Palatul National, the Parliament of Moldova, the National Museum of History (with a Soviet helicopter on its front lawn!) and the National Opera and Theatre, in front of which you can grab a nice selfie with the “I Love Chisinau” sign. The Stephen the Great monument also seemed to attract many locals for selfies and photo opportunities as well.

Stefan cel Mare si Sfant Boulevard

The main thoroughfare in Chisinau is “Stefan cel Mare si Sfant Boulevard”, a wide avenue which primarily contains shops and high rise apartment blocks. At the very end of this avenue, you will see the Soviet styled “Chisinau Hotel” as well as the beautiful golden domed Ciuflea Monastery with its powder blue exterior and stunning interior and extravagant artwork. A further 20 minute walk from here takes you to the Eternity Memorial Complex - a military memorial with a soaring red pyramid of 5 rifles encircling the eternal flame.

Finally, well worth the walk is a visit to the abandoned Chisinau Soviet State Circus. If you like Communist-era architecture, this will not disappoint. For me, this is one of the most eye catching buildings in Chisinau - catch it while you can, it may not survive much longer!

Chisinau is a very, very cheap city for eating and drinking. The main beer is called “Chisinau” and beside the hotel, there are ample places to sit outside on the terrace and people watch or sit inside. As a solo traveller, I felt very safe walking around on my own. Many people only speak very basic English. Moldova is known for its excellent red wine, from the Cricova winery, which you can pick up at the airport duty free or on the flight home. I definitely suggest you try it!
