Zagreb

Zagreb, Croatia
09/18
Croatia Airlines, direct from Dublin (DUB) to Zagreb (ZAG)

Zagreb

When you think of Croatia, you automatically think Dubrovnik or Split both located on the stunning Adriatic Sea. However, I wanted to avoid the crowds of tourists and decided to try out the Croatian capital : Zagreb. And I can assure you that I was not disappointed!

Croatia is in the EU and uses its own currency which is called the Hryvnia. You can pick this up in Dublin Airport before your flight at the currency exchange counter.

Direct flights between Dublin and Zagreb are available with Croatia Airlines, 3 times weekly on Tuesdays, Thursdays and Sundays. I went for a 3 night break and decided to book an apartment using booking.com. I stayed at Mango Apartman, Builceva 7, Zagreb. The apartment/studio was incredibly clean and the owner even organised a free taxi to collect me at the airport. I paid €180 for 3 nights and it was absolutely perfect. The host was super friendly and I would have absolutely no hesitation to stay there again. I would highly recommend it, just a 5 minute walk into the main city square.

As Zagreb is a pretty small capital by international standards, you can walk absolutely everywhere. No need to use any of the trams or any public transport.

The Main Square (Trg Bana Josip) is the focal point of the city and you should orient yourself based on its location. Behind this square and only a 5 minute stroll away, you will find the beautiful Zagreb Cathedral with its tall twin spires and a very lively Market (Dolac Market) with stalls and stands selling everything from fruit and veg, locally produced honey, ornaments and replica (fake) Croatia soccer jerseys!

Next on your list about a 10 minute walk from here should be the Museum of Broken Relationships. A pretty interesting if not bizarre museum which contains various mementos of doomed and failed romances. Haven’t seen anything quite like this before, so well worth heading inside even if it is some respite out of the sun!

Further ahead, you can walk towards the stunning St Marks Church with its beautiful coloured tiled roof which display the emblems of Croatia and Zagreb. Have a look inside, but it is definitely the exterior of this church which is most impressive. In this square, you will also find the “Sabor” which is the Parliament Building as well as the Presidential Palace. This square is definitely an excellent spot for some selfies!

For the best views in Zagreb, you can take a walk up the Lotrscak Tower where you can enjoy some fine 360-degree views over the entire city. Every day at noon, a canon is fired from the top of this tower as crowds gather below. The noise is deafening and can certainly catch you off guard, so be aware!

The main strip where you will find the majority of Pubs/Restaurants is called “Ul Ivana Tkalcica”. Definitely a people watching haven, you can grab a coffee during the day or stop by for a cocktail and go bar crawling and enjoy the atmosphere at night. My main go-to was the Croatian Beers which are extremely cheap: “Karlovacko” and “Ozujsko” the main beer brands that I would recommend.

Zagreb is a clean, safe and very lively city with a buzzing atmosphere. I am definitely going to go back and the Croats are always so friendly and welcoming. It is a cheap city, plenty of options for dining depending on your budget and everywhere is within walking distance. Give Zagreb a try, I think you will enjoy it! :)
